### (a) Value of the overlay (pure-index, state→allocation channel)
- Full 2000-now +0.79pp; **2003+ (drop nascent, per user) +1.10pp**; PRE14 2003-13 +1.78pp;
  MODERN 2014+ +0.54pp. The 2001 nascent-market episode (<5 listed cos, +31% missed) was
  dragging the full number — removing it lifts the edge to +1.10pp, confirming it was noise.
- **Value is overwhelmingly crisis-driven, not steady alpha**:
  - 2011 inflation window: DT4 −13.5% → DT5G **+2.7% (+16.2pp)** — turned a losing year positive.
  - 2008 GFC window: DT4 +7.5% → DT5G +13.8% (+6.4pp).
  - These two crises ARE the overlay. In a crisis-free stretch (2014+) it is a modest +0.5pp.
  → "Insurance, not alpha" confirmed WITH magnitudes. The de-risk arm earns its keep.

### (b) Easing-off ablation — NUANCED, do NOT disable outright
- Net full-history: caps-only (NOEASE) is marginally BETTER (+0.94 vs +0.79 full; +1.26 vs
  +1.10 on 2003+) — but the entire advantage comes from PRE-2014.
- **The easing arm's bad record is a PRE-MATURITY artifact**: 2012 easing era it cost −3.4pp
  (premature re-entry into the still-falling 2012 market; PRE14 NOEASE +0.68pp better).
- **In the matured 2014+ regime the easing arm is net POSITIVE: DT5G +0.54 vs NOEASE +0.27
  (+0.27pp)** — the 2014 SBV-easing re-entries (T+60 +11%) were good calls.

  failure belongs to the same unreliable pre-2013 regime as the 2001 signal. → KEEP the
  easing arm for the modern regime; disabling it only helps by discarding pre-2014 history
  we already distrust. Earlier "disable easing" call is REVISED to "keep; it's fine post-2014".

### Bottom line

fine in the modern regime (+0.27pp), only looked bad in the discounted pre-2013 era.
